What is hostage negotation?

Negotiation is one of the most delicate processes in a hostage recovery mission. In some cases it is the only needed contact with the hostage takers, but in others it is only used to stall while the tactical element takes over. "Priorities in a hostage incident include preservation of life, apprehension of the HT, and recovering or protecting property".(Fuselier,1981). Many times hostage taking is considered an act of terrorism and is analyzed from the perspectives of foreign and domestic influences, the police tactical response, the motivation for capitulation(capture), and the value of negotiation.(Miller,1980). The job of hostage negotiators is to slowly dissolve the situation while saving lives. But sometimes negotiation isn't enough and the use of brute forces is needed to save innocent life and may use lethal force on the hostage takers.

What should I do first? What is ICER? What should I start with? How does hostage negotiation begin?

Isolation

Isolate the area where the incident is taking place; creating a safe zone in case something goes wrong and keeping civilians out of this area.

Contain

Limit the capabilities of the hostage taker and contain them within the inner perimeter of the safe zone. Also keep them from seeing what the officers are doing if possible.

Evaluate

Evaluate all the intelligence available, this initial information can come from the hostage taker or an intermediary (a hostage sent to talk to the police), or a witness of the events leading to the hostage situation. Try to figure out the amount of hostages, injuries, weapons, motive, etc. without escalating or provoking the situation. After this information is gathered the head officer can decide what the next step is.

Report

Report all available information gathered, including on the hostage takers and hostages, events, weapons, zones, routes and so on, informing other officers and the chain of command. It is very important that the on scene officer is aware of this process to contain the situation, prevent escalation of the hostage situation, and make the job easier for the negotiators so they can defuse the situation a quickly as possible. If all of these are done correctly the hostage negotiator can assess the situation and do his job smoothly.

How can I get close to the hostage taker? Getting close.

Creating normality

Whilst there may be chaos and panic on all sides, the negotiator first seeks to create calm. They talk in a calm voice and do a great deal of listening. In particular, they seek to establish a sense of normality among the emotion, a space in which the hostage-takers can talk with the negotiator as reasonable people, much as you would talk with any normal person on the phone. The negotiator is always there and always ready to talk. They will listen to everything and will create an even keel on which reasonable negotiation can be conducted.

Creating Humanity

Within the normality, the negotiator listens uncritically to the hostage-taker, accepting them as they are and creating a sense of humanity. From that humanity, they then can extend to discussing the hostages, how they are bearing up and whether they are unwell.

Developing authority

The negotiator may also seek to position themselves as an authority figure. This can start by being authoritative on behalf of the hostage-taker, for example in getting them communications and food. This may later turn to being authoritative with the hostage-taker, which can be a tricky and dangerous activity as the hostage-taker wants to be in charge. Authoritative work may thus be done in particular circumstances. If the negotiators can establish this relationship, they may be able to direct the hostage-taker's actions more effectively.

How do I progress in a hostage situation? What are some good first steps?

Small steps

since trust is still being built small steps are required. It can range anywhere from giving them food to talking about football for a little while. The total amount of conversation may be adjusted according to the urgency. If the hostage-takers are requiring transport or other things that would lead to more problems, then it may be more prudent to insert delays, such as saying you are 'looking into it'

Managing Stress

Stress and tension will be ever present in these situations. This will complicate the situation even further unless dealt with properly. It is the negotiators job to make sure that the stress stays at a reasonable level and does not get out of control.

Exploring Situations

Eventually they can start discussing ways to end the situation. This needs to be done with extreme care as to not provoke the hostage taker to end communication. This could either immediate the release if the hostages or end poorly resulting in the injury or death of hostages. Sometimes the only thing the hostage taker wants is to have publicity and that is easily achievable.
